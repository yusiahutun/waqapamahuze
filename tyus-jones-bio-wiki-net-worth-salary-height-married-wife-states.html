Who is Tyus Robert Jones? His Bio & Wiki

Tyus Jones was named NCAA Tournament MVP after leading the Duke Blue Devils to an NCAA Championship in 2015. Moreover, he was taken 24th overall in the 2015 NBA Draft by the Cleveland Cavaliers, then traded to the Minnesota Timberwolves. In a highly anticipated announcement, Jones and his close friend, Jahlil Okafor dual-committed to Duke University on November 15, 2013.

Jones was born as Tyus Robert Jones on May 10, 1996, in Burnsville, Minnesota, U.S. Moreover, Jones’ nationality is American and his ethnicity is African-American. He was raised along with Jadee, Tre, and Reggie by their parents, Debbie and Rob Jones, both are basketball players. Unfortunately, his parents divorced due to irreconcilable differences between them.

Concerning about Jones’ education, he attended Apple Valley High School where he turned his focus towards basketball. Later, he joined Duke University men’s basketball team in 2014 and played for the Cleveland Cavaliers.

Inside of Tyus Jones’ Career

Talking about Tyus Jones’ profession, Jones started his career playing college basketball for the Duke Blue Devils in his freshman season as part of the 2014–2015 National Championship team. Moreover, he was a 2014 USA Today second-team All-USA Boys Basketball Team selection. At Duke, Jones was an All-ACC third team and All-ACC Freshman 1st team selection.

Furthermore, Jones earned NCAA Basketball Tournament Most Outstanding Player during Duke’s victory in the championship game of the 2015 NCAA Men’s Division I Basketball Tournament. Subsequently, Jones announced he would enter the 2015 NBA draft. Moreover, he was selected with the 24th overall pick by the Cleveland Cavaliers and traded to the Minnesota Timberwolves.

Jones is well known for being the part of the 2014–2015 National Championship team. In 2014 FIBA Americas Under-18 Championship, Jones was named the captain of the US national team and won a gold medal for his country.
