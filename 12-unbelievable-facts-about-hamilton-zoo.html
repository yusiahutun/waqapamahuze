The Hamilton Zoo is home to over 600 animals.

With a wide variety of species, including lions, tigers, giraffes, and monkeys, the Hamilton Zoo offers visitors a chance to see an impressive collection of wildlife up close and personal.

Hamilton Zoo has been open since 1969.

For over 50 years, Hamilton Zoo has been providing a fun and educational experience for both locals and tourists. It has become a beloved attraction in the area.

Hamilton Zoo is actively involved in conservation efforts.

The zoo plays a crucial role in the conservation of endangered species through breeding programs, research, and educational initiatives. They strive to protect and preserve biodiversity.

It is the only zoo in New Zealand to have rhinoceroses.

Hamilton Zoo is proud to house rhinoceroses, an endangered species that they have worked hard to support and protect. Visitors can learn about the conservation efforts being made to save these majestic creatures.

The zoo offers close encounters with some of the animals.

For a truly unforgettable experience, visitors have the opportunity to get up close and personal with certain animals at the zoo. From feeding giraffes to interacting with meerkats, these encounters create lasting memories.

Hamilton Zoo boasts a beautiful walkthrough aviary.

Visitors can immerse themselves in the world of colorful birds as they stroll through the walkthrough aviary. It’s a peaceful and serene experience that allows for a closer connection with nature.

The zoo hosts a range of educational programs.

Hamilton Zoo is dedicated to educating visitors of all ages about the importance of wildlife conservation. They offer informative talks, guided tours, and interactive exhibits to engage and inspire people to protect our planet’s precious biodiversity.

There is a dedicated children’s playground within the zoo.

Hamilton Zoo is a family-friendly destination, and the addition of a dedicated children’s playground ensures that young visitors have a fun and engaging experience during their visit.

Hamilton Zoo organizes special events throughout the year.

From themed days to holiday celebrations, the zoo always has something exciting happening. These events provide additional value and entertainment for visitors of all ages.

FAQs

1. What are the operating hours of Hamilton Zoo?

Hamilton Zoo is open every day from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm, including weekends and public holidays.

2. Do I need to purchase tickets in advance?

It is recommended to purchase your tickets in advance to save time and guarantee entry, especially during peak season or weekends.

3. Is there parking available at Hamilton Zoo?

Yes, there is ample parking available at the zoo. Parking fees may apply.

4. Are there any guided tours available?

Yes, Hamilton Zoo offers guided tours led by knowledgeable staff. These tours provide a deeper understanding of the animals, their habitats, and the conservation efforts at the zoo.

5. Can I bring my own food and drinks?

Yes, you are allowed to bring your own food and drinks to Hamilton Zoo. There are designated picnic areas where you can enjoy your refreshments.

6. Are there any feeding opportunities for visitors?

Yes, Hamilton Zoo offers various feeding opportunities for visitors, allowing them to hand-feed select animals under the supervision of zoo staff.

7. Is Hamilton Zoo wheelchair accessible?

Yes, Hamilton Zoo is wheelchair accessible, with ramps and designated paths throughout the facility.

8. Are there any dining options available within the zoo?

Yes, Hamilton Zoo has a café where you can enjoy a range of food and beverages.

9. Can I bring my pet to Hamilton Zoo?

No, pets are not allowed within the zoo for the safety and well-being of both the animals and the visitors.

10. Does Hamilton Zoo have any educational programs?

Yes, Hamilton Zoo offers educational programs for school groups, families, and individuals, aimed at promoting conservation awareness and understanding of wildlife.
