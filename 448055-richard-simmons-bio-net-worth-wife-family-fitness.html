Early life

Richard Simmons was born on July 12, 1948, in New Orleans, Louisiana, USA. His full name is Milton Teagle Simmons. The fitness personality adopted the name Richard after an uncle who paid for his college tuition. Richard’s father, Leonard Douglas Simmons Sr. Leonard was raised as a Methodist and worked as a master of ceremonies and later in thrift stores.

Richard’s mother was Shirley May (née Satin). She was Russian Jewish and worked as a travelling fan dancer who later became a store cosmetics saleswoman. The fitness guru was raised in the French Quarter of New Orleans with his elder brother.

Simmons left the Methodist church at an early age and converted to Catholicism. Consequently, he attended Cor Jesu High School. After high school, he was enrolled at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette and later graduated from Florida State University with a Bachelor’s Degree in Art.

Fitness journey

While he was a child, Richard’s first job in New Orleans was selling pralines at Leah's Pralines. However, he wanted to become a priest, which did not happen. In the 1970s, he relocated to Los Angeles. While there, Simmons worked as the maître d'hôtel at Derek's Restaurant in Beverly Hills.

It was while working here that Richard developed an interest in body fitness. The gyms available provided day services, therefore not favouring many unhealthy persons. This led Richard to establish gyms, and through his interest in fitness, he lost 123 lb (56 kg).

Richard later opened his exercise studio, which he named The Anatomy Asylum. The studio emphasized healthy eating in proper portions and enjoyable exercise in a supportive atmosphere. To start with, the studio started with a salad bar restaurant known as Ruffage

He later removed the restaurant as the focus of the Asylum studio majored in exercise. Richard later renamed the studio Slimmons. Through the studio, he taught motivational classes and aerobics throughout the week. However, Slimmons closed in November 2016.

During the early 1980s, Simmons hosted two popular shows, namely Slim Cookin and The Richard Simmons Show. His focus was on personal health, fitness, exercise, and healthy cooking in the shows.

The Richard Simmons Show attracted many exercise enthusiasts, including actress Lucrecia Sarita Russo, who transported a bus full of women for a workout on the show. The show bagged an Emmy Award.
