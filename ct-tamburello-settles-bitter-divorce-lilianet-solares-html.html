The Challenge's C.T. Tamburello has settled his bitter divorce from his ex-wife, Lilianet Solares.

The 43-year-old reality television personality and his former spouse reached a settlement earlier this month and have agreed to split custody of their son Christopher, seven, according to TMZ. 

The media figure, who revealed that he had lost nearly 70 pounds during his divorce proceedings, will also have to pay $761.39 in child support each month, as well as $332.45 for each week he is not able to be with his child.

Although Tamburello will keep the couple's house, he will pay Solares $30,000 for her part of the property's equity.

The MTV star will remain in possession of his company, New Leaf Investments, and his former wife also retains ownership of Solares Management.

The reality television personality and his former wife have maintained an on-off relationship for several years, and they welcomed their son in 2016.

The couple's 2018 wedding was captured in the two-part series CT's Getting Married, which aired on MTV that year.

Tamburello and Solares briefly separated during the course of their marriage, although they later resumed their relationship.

One of the pair's emotional phone calls were featured on an episode of The Challenge: Spies, Lies & Allies, which aired in 2021.

TMZ revealed that the the former couple had decided to begin divorce proceedings in November of last year. 

Tamburello vented about his split from Solares in a video that was shared on his Instagram account this past April, and according to People, he stated that he was 'tired of getting' dragged' by his former partner.

The reality television personality also issued a message to his ex-wife, and bemoaned the way she had been treating him in the recent past.

'I can honestly say I've given you everything you asked for, including this divorce,' he stated.

The media figure then accused Solares of 'hiding money' from him, and stated that her new boyfriend, whom he described as 'a f***ing stripper from Magic Mike,' was likely paying all of her bills.

Tamburello also alleged that his former partner was 'making 70 grand a year,' which she withheld from her family. 

'It wasn't until we got the financial statements that we realized that me and [our son], CJ, were her secret life,' he stated.

The reality television personality concluded by expressing that he wanted to focus on the well-being of his son and start a new life as 'a stay-at-home dad.'
