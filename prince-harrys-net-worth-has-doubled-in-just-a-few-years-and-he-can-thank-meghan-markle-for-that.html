Prince Harry's net worth has doubled in just a few years and he can thank Meghan Markle for that

Prince Harry tied the knot with Meghan Markle in 2018, and since then there have been huge changes to his life, and net worth. In fact, it’s actually doubled since then.

Ever since leaving the royal family in 2020, Prince Harry and Meghan Markle stopped being working royals and went on to embark on their own ventures. This included their bombshell Netflix documentary, and the Duke writing his memoir Spare, to name a few. The Duchess is also gearing up to start her lifestyle brand, American Riviera Orchard. Although Harry has gotten a lot of stick from those back home in the UK, it looks like he hasn’t suffered, at least, his bank account hasn’t.

Prince Harry’s net worth has skyrocketed from 2017 to now

Prince Harry and Meghan Markle first met in July 2016, when he was 32. Meghan is three years his senior and was 35 at the time.

In 2017, the year after they met, the Prince’s net worth was estimated to be $37.3 million (£30 million) according to The Richest. Both he and Prince William gathered most of their wealth on their 30th birthdays, which included an inheritance of $16.1 million (£13 million) in trust and estate from their late mother Princess Diana.

By 2018, the Richest reported both brothers had a net worth of $34.8 million (£28 million).

In 2019, the celebrity couple were well and truly settled together, and as the former Suits actress came in with a net worth of $5 million, together, the pair were worth an estimated $45 million.

Cut to 2022, everything was starting to return to normal after the pandemic, and the Sussexes were settled into their new life in California. “I’ve got all my mom left me, and without that we would not be able to do this,” Harry told Oprah about their new life.

Their company, Archewell Productions, signed a deal with Netflix that was estimated to be around $100 – $150 million. Although the Duke’s memoir was released in 2023, it was reported by Page Six that he received an advance of $20 million.

They also signed a $20 million deal with Spotify, however, one author claimed they were only given a portion of this.

The Duke’s net worth has doubled since 2017

After all the ups and downs, it’s now reported the couple are worth $60 million, almost double what Harry was worth in 2017.

Meghan’s net worth has also vastly increased since marrying her husband, as the pair now share their money and often take on projects together.

After marrying into the family, she received a 91-piece jewelry collection worth $747,000 (£600,000). And it doesn’t look like her net worth will be declining any time soon. With the release of the lifestyle brand American Riviera Orchard just around the corner, it looks like the only way is up for the Sussexes’ bank accounts.
