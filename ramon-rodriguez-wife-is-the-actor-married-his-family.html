Ramón Rodríguez Family

Ramón Rodríguez’s parents are folks from Santurce, Puerto Rico according to thefamilynation.com. His mother is Maria Monserrate Rodriguez Bello and it is she who introduced Ramón to Salsa dancing back in 2001. (He later received training in Salsa from a Latin Dance Company in New York.)

While Ramón appears to have a close relationship with his mother, he talked about “a very complicated sort of relationship” with his musician father. Back in August 2020, he told Variety that his complicated relationship with his dad has come a long way because of a script that he wrote loosely based on their relationship.

Ramón has three older sisters and also three younger brothers (who are living in Puerto Rico). He said that this script has “actually” helped him get past a lot of it, “a lot of the trauma”.

Speaking of siblings, Ramón has six, three older sisters and three younger brothers. Among them is brother Edgardo Miranda-Rodriguez, who is a writer, artist, and graphic novelist. Edgardo wrote once on his Facebook that his mother Maria raised his brother Axel and his sister Marisol and himself on her own.

Ramón Rodríguez Accent

Ramón Rodríguez speaks with a slight, southern accent.

He describes himself as a New Yorker, Puerto Rican who grew up on the Lower East Side of Manhattan.

How Much Is Ramón Rodríguez Net Worth?

Ramón never really thought of becoming an actor. It was a mere coincidence that kind of happened through basketball. He used to play the game and even had gotten a scholarship in Michigan at age 14, through it. One day so it happened that he was invited to a Nike competition by a friend where he had to do tricks. He had gone to the competition because they were giving away free sneakers. He ended up winning this tricks competition, and Nike asked him to be a part of their commercials and a bunch of other spots. So, that was him encountering the camera for the first time. He then started doing other roles in TV and films and even took classes for them. With time, he started appreciating the craft and so he also naturally fell in love with it.

Ramón Rodríguez Education

It appears Ramón Rodríguez never received any degree or formal training in Acting. Here’s what we know about his education.

He first went to the New York City Lab School for Collaborative Studies before transferring to The Leelanau School in Michigan. Eventually, he also found himself playing basketball at Wheeling Jesuit University in West Virginia for two years. Only then did he got enrolled at New York University in a Sports Marketing program.
