Josh McDaniels Career Highlights

Josh McDaniels’ journey through the world of football has been nothing short of remarkable.

It all began during his high school years at Canton McKinley High School, where he honed his skills as a wide receiver.

While attending John Carroll University, McDaniels continued his football journey, showcasing his talents on the field.

The coaching chapter of his career commenced in 1999 when he took on the role of a graduate assistant at Michigan State University, where he laid the foundation for his coaching expertise.

In 2001, Josh McDaniels made a significant transition to the NFL when he joined the New England Patriots as a personnel assistant.

His tenure with the Patriots saw him wear multiple coaching hats, starting as a defensive coaching assistant from 2002 to 2003.

Subsequently, he assumed the crucial role of quarterbacks coach from 2004 to 2008, contributing to the team’s successes.

McDaniels further elevated his status within the Patriots organization by becoming the offensive coordinator from 2006 to 2008, playing a pivotal role in the team’s offensive strategies.

His expertise extended beyond the Patriots, as he ventured into the head coaching role with the Denver Broncos from 2009 to 2010.

In 2011, McDaniels broadened his horizons, serving as the offensive coordinator for the St. Louis Rams before reuniting with the New England Patriots as an offensive assistant.

At the time of his initial hiring as an NFL head coach, McDaniels earned the distinction of being the league’s youngest head coach, assuming the role at the tender age of 33.

In 2022, McDaniels embarked on a new chapter in his coaching career by accepting the head coaching position with the Las Vegas Raiders, marking a significant transition from his long-standing association with the New England Patriots.

Raiders Firing

In a surprising turn of events, the Las Vegas Raiders have made the decision to part ways with head coach Josh McDaniels and general manager David Ziegler, as officially announced late Tuesday night.

Owner Mark Davis shared his perspective on the matter, stating, “After much contemplation regarding the direction the Raiders need to take, I have made the difficult decision to separate from Josh and Dave. I extend my appreciation to both of them for their dedicated efforts and wish them and their families nothing but success in their future endeavors.”

The interim head coach position is expected to be filled by linebackers coach Antonio Pierce, according to sources reporting to ESPN’s Adam Schefter.

When contacted by ESPN, Mark Davis chose not to offer any further insights into the decision.

These significant changes in leadership come on the heels of the Raiders’ 26-14 loss to the Detroit Lions on “Monday Night Football,” a defeat that pushed the team’s record to 3-5 for the current season.

Josh McDaniels had been at the helm of the Raiders for his second season, having signed a six-year contract in January 2022.

Notably, the franchise still has over four full years remaining on the contract, which Las Vegas is obligated to fulfill.

McDaniels departs from his role with the Raiders, leaving behind a record of 9 wins and 16 losses, without any playoff appearances during his tenure.

Josh McDaniels’ Father, Thom McDaniels

Josh’s father, Thom McDaniels, also coached football for high schools in Ohio before retiring. 

Thom held the position of head coach for 26 years, spending time at Massillon Jackson High School, Canton McKinley High School, and Warren G. Harding High School.

Similarly, Thom also coached for 40 years in total and was inducted into the Ohio high school coaches’ Hall of Fame.

In 2004 and 2010, Thom was a finalist twice for the NFL High School Coach of the Year. 

Additionally, in 1997, while coaching his Canton McKinley team to state and national championships, Thom was voted USA Today’s national high school football coach of the year. This was the final year of his first stint as Canton McKinley’s head coach. 

Thom mows greens on a golf course these days. However, over his 40 years as a high school football coach in northeast Ohio, he carved out a legend.

Josh’s father doesn’t hold back when he thinks his son needs to hear anything like most good fathers do.

When Josh was five years old, he first went to his practices with his father, and even now, Josh agrees with his father whenever he has something to say about coaching. 

Apart from Josh himself, Thom coached several notable players, including Percy Snow, Maurice Clarett, Mike Doss, and Mario Manningham.

Josh McDaniels’ Brother, Ben McDaniels

Ben McDaniels, Josh’s younger brother, was born on June 6, 1980. He currently serves as the wide receivers coach and passing game coordinator for the National Football League’s Houston Texans (NFL).

Prior to late 2018, Ben held the positions of interim wide receivers coach and quarterbacks coach at the University of Michigan.

Ralph Friedgen opted to step down as offensive coordinator following the season, which led to Ben being elevated to wide receivers coach for the Rutgers Scarlet Knights football team.

In 2010, Ben worked as a quarterback assistant with the National Football League’s Denver Broncos. From 2000 to 2001, he competed as a quarterback for Kent State.

Josh McDaniels: Videotaping Scandal

On November 27, 2010, The Denver Post reported that the NFL had launched an investigation into the Denver Broncos, prompted by a troubling revelation.

It came to light that Steve Scarnecchia, the team’s director of video operations, who was initially hired by Josh McDaniels in 2009, had surreptitiously recorded a walkthrough practice of the San Francisco 49ers during the Broncos’ Week 8 game at Wembley Stadium.

In swift response, the NFL imposed a $50,000 fine on both the Broncos and Josh McDaniels on the same day.

The incident also led to the termination of Steve Scarnecchia’s employment with the team.

As the investigation unfolded and under the scrutiny of NFL investigators, Scarnecchia confessed to acting independently and acknowledged that he understood the impropriety of his actions in recording the walkthrough practice.

In the course of the inquiry, NFL security professionals conducted a forensic examination of the computer from which Scarnecchia had subsequently deleted the incriminating tape.

Following the comprehensive investigation, both the NFL and the Denver Broncos concurred that Josh McDaniels had been unaware of the incident.

However, the NFL still penalized McDaniels for failing to promptly notify the league office of the occurrence, as stipulated by league regulations.

Furthermore, due to the principle that “clubs are ultimately liable for the conduct of their personnel,” the NFL also imposed penalties on the Broncos as an organization.
