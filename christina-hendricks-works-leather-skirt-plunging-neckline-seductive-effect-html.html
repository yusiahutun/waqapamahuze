She never disappoints with her sultry appearance on the 1960s AMC hit Mad Men.

And Christina Hendricks certainly knows how to work her curves to equally  traffic-stopping effect in her own modern attire.

The 37-year-old stepped out in New York yesterday working a skintight burgundy leather skirt and a black silk top with a dangerously low neckline.

Christina accentuated her toned calves with high black heels, and carried an elegant black clutch.

The actress kept her make-up subtle but interesting, with a light shimmer of green eyeshadow, rosy cheeks, and a hint of pink lipstick.

Christina's accessories were simple, with the focus on one intricate necklace.

She recently revealed a saucy secret as to how she gets into character for her role as as office manager Joan Harris in Mad Men.

'We have the full undergarments and the slips and the thing over that,' she divulged.

In a recent interview with AMC - the network which airs the critically acclaimed drama -  Christina discussed how fortunate she feels playing her character.

Getting into character: Christina recently revealed that wearing the proper lingerie is a great help

The star chatted about the new season of the show, and her reaction to co-star January Jones wearing a 'fat' suit as character Betty Francis.

'I think I've been really lucky in that Joan is really a one-of-a-kind character,' she said.

'She's not a stereotype and you don't just see roles like that in every script you get. People have asked me if I've worried about being typecast, but there is no "type" for Joan.'

She also said the most shocking part of the current fifth season was seeing Betty Francis (January Jones) as 'fat.'

'Betty being fat. I mean I think that's a shocker for everyone,' she said. January [Jones] was so game and thought it was a really cool storytelling technique and so she was enthusiastic about it and a true sport.'
