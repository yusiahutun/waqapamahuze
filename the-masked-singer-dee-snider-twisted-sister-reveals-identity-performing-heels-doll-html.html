The Masked Singer: Dee Snider of Twisted Sister reveals identity after performing in heels as Doll

Twisted Sister singer Dee Snider was unmasked after performing as Doll on Wednesday's episode of The Masked Singer's on Fox.

The 68-year-old singer, who wore high heels and a dress to go with his creepy mask that had a button for an eye and a mouth stapled shut, told the audience on WB Movie Night that he just had one thing to say after receiving the lowest score.

'We're Not Going to Take It!' he bellowed while singing the words to his hit song. The audience and the panelists joined in.

'Thank you, everyone,' Dee said.

Before his unmasking Dee as Doll shared clues to his identity that included Elvis Presley, a lollipop and a playbill from the show Guys and Dolls. He then took the stage and performed Jailhouse Rock as dancers with popcorn hats moved around him.

'He rocked it like a pro that has been doing this forever,' Jenny McCarthy, 50, said. 'It was so incredible.'

Show host Nick Cannon, 42, told them to bring out another clue that turned out to be a guitar case with the label 'King Status.'

'Besides making movies, the King and I shared making platinum records,' Doll said.

Robin Thicke, 46, thought it was Gene Simmons of Kiss. Ken Jeong, 53, guessed it was Austin Butler, who played Elvis in the recent Oscar-nominated movie, and the crowd booed him. Nicole Scherzinger, 44, thought it might be rockstar David Lee Roth. Jenny thought it might be Iggy Pop. They all turned out to be wrong.

'I've guessed him so many times on this show,' Jenny said.

'I forgot you were so tall,' Robin said.

'The heels help,' Dee said. 'But let me tell you, Gene Simmons, Iggy Pop, no one could work pumps like I do.'

Robin said he was a huge fan. Ken told him he was a legend.

Doll costume: The 68-year-old singer wore high heels and a dress to go with his creepy mask that had a button for an eye and a mouth stapled shut

Each of the panelists also dressed up in outfits to go with the episode's WB movies theme. Jenny dressed up as a sultry movie star in a pink dress with matching pink gloves. Ken dressed as his character Leslie Chow from the Hangover films. Nicole posed as a replicant from the movie Blade Runner and Robin dressed as James Dean from Rebel Without A Cause.

Dandelion, a green costume with a giant floral head, was the second performer. Dandelion's clues included that she had worked with Madonna, Flea, Elton John, a tornado, and an apple. She sang Judy Garland's Over The Rainbow and her head and her costume seemed to light up when she sang.

'She just flew over that rainbow and beyond,' Nicole said.

'Honestly, absolutely gorgeous,' Jenny said.

Nick had a new clue brought out of ruby slippers with a tag on them that read 'Billboards.'

'These heels were made for performing,' Dandelion said. 'TV, movies, concerts and even charting next to Olivia Rodrigo.'

Nicole guessed it was Zoey Deschanel. Jenny disagreed and thought it was Emmy Rossum. Ken insisted it was Reese Witherspoon and the crowd booed him again.

'He's crazy,' Nicole said. 'He's gone off his rocker.'

Flowering plant: Dandelion, a green costume with a giant floral head, sang Judy Garland's Over The Rainbow

A small boy held up a handwritten sign that said 'Go Ken' encouraging him. The panelist then also participated in a fun sketch acting like they were fighting in The Matrix.

Mantis, a giant green bug with wings wearing a leather jacket and jeans, came out next. His clues were that he was a comic, a published author and wanted to dance with the wolves. Mantis sang Bob Seger's Old Time Rock 'N Roll.

'Amazing!' Nicole said.

'I loved that. That was my dad's all time favorite party song. Loved the performance,' Robin said referencing his late father Alan Thicke who died in 2016 at age 69.

'You are a performer,' Jenny said.

Mantis' additional clue turned out to be a pair of sunglasses with a tag that read 'true story.'

'Representing a legend was instrumental to my success and it was an honor to do so,' Mantis said.

Robin thought it was Dennis Quaid. Jenny thought it was Kevin Bacon. Ken insisted it was Bruce Springsteen.

Rocking out: Mantis, a giant green bug with wings wearing a leather jacket and jeans, sang Bob Seger's Old Time Rock 'N Roll

For the final Battle Royale, Dandelion and Mantis went head-to-head singing (I've Got) a Golden Ticket, a song from Willy Wonka & the Chocolate Factory.

'Dandelion, your voice was epic,' Jenny said. 'It's such a toss up.'

'They both know exactly what they are doing up there,' Nicole said.

After the votes were tabulated, Dandelion was declared the winner and moved on in the competition. The crowd cheered for Mantis to unmask.

But right before the Mantis was about to unmask, Robin ran up and rang the Keep It On bell to save him.

'From one man to another, Mantis,' Robin said.

'You live to sing another day, Mantis,' Nick said.

The crowd chanted 'ding dong keep it on.'

'Wow, it's because you like the wardrobe choices, right' Mantis said.

'No, I'll tell you, straight truth, it was your song choices,' Robin said. 'The first song, Old Time Rock 'N Roll, was literally my father's favorite song. He performed it at home at parties. That song got to my heart. And then your performance in the Battle Royal was even stronger. My 12-year-old son played Charlie in the Willy Wonka play, and he sang that song just months ago onstage. So I cannot let you go when you sing my dad's song and my son's song.'

The Masked Singer will return next Wednesday on Fox.
