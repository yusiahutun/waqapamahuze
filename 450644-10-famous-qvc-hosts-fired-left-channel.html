10 most famous QVC hosts who were fired or left the channel

Quality Value Convenience (QVC) is an American free-to-air television network and flagship shopping channel, specialising in televised home shopping. Over the years, the network has used the services of several employees to achieve its objectives. However, like any other work setup, employees leave or get fired for various reasons. So, who are the QVC hosts who were fired?

QVC hosts wow fans with their impressive presentation skills. As a result, they tend to develop a bond with their audience. Therefore, when a host leaves or is fired by the network, their fans are left disappointed.

Who are the QVC hosts who were fired or left the channel?

You may have noticed that some of your favourite QVC hosts are no longer presenting. What happened to them? Here is a list of hosts who have at one point anchored the home shopping channel but are no longer part of it.

1. Judy Crowell

  • Full name: Judy Crowell
  • Place of birth: Tokyo, Japan
  • Occupation: Host

Crowell was one of the first faces to be hired on QVC. She left in 2003, after more than a decade of helping viewers make sense of the complicated world of shopping. She quit without giving any reason.

Judy allegedly left QCV because of her marriage with her colleague of six years at QVC, Paul Deasy, in 2003. She moved to HSN and worked there before eventually living in 2008. She turned in 2015 as a host for ShopNBC.

She hosted alongside her husband. They have a delightful onscreen partnership that viewers have grown to love.

5. Bob Bowersox

7. Jacque Gonzalez
