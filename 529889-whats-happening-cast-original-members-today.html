What's Happening cast now: Where are the original members today?

Many curious fans have been wondering about the whereabouts of the What's Happening cast after the show's cancellation. The ABC sitcom series ran from 5th August 1976 to 28th April 1979, detailing the lives of three working-class African-American teenagers living in Watts, Los Angeles.

Ernest Lee Thomas was born on 26th March 1949 in Indiana Gary, US and became a high-profile TV star after he departed from What's Happening. He landed acting roles in films such as A Piece of the Action, The Brady Bunch Hour, Baretta, and Roots.

2. (Dwayne Nelson) Haywood Nelson

3. (Rerun Stubbs)Frederick Berry

Frederick Allen Berry was born on 19th March 1951 in St. Louis, Missouri, United States. He was often touted as the clown of the What's Happening cast, earning him the nickname Rerun on the show. 

He kept a low profile after the cancellation of the ABC show, but he returned to feature on the What's Happening Now sequel. He was eventually fired after requesting a pay rise that was subsequently higher than his co-stars.

Stubbs allegedly struggled with drug and substance abuse that greatly impacted his overall health. He was found dead in his Los Angeles apartment in 2003, and his apparent cause of death was due to natural causes.

4. (Dee Thomas) Danielle Spencer

Danielle Spenser was born on 24th June 1965 in North Trenton, New Jersey, and was renowned for her role on What's Happening. She played the role of Dee Thomas, the rude little girl who had an affinity love for money, and she would even resort to blackmail to cash in.

6. (Shirley) Shirley Hemphill

Shirley Hemphill was born on 1st July 1947 in Asheville, North Carolina, US. She played the role of a waitress at Rob's place on What's Happening. Aside from appearing in Martin after ABC's show cancellation, the waitress went on to land her sitcom, One In a Million.

7. Eric Monte

Eric Monte is the producer of What's Happening. He was born on 25th December 1943 in Chicago, Illinois. Eric is the creative brainchild behind What's Happening, its sequel, What's Happening Now, and another subsequent film, the Good Times.

As the producer of What's Happening, he auditioned and assembled the show's casting crew. However, in 1977, he filed a lawsuit against CBS and Tandem Productions for alleged copyright and intellectual theft.

Monte allegedly received a $1 million compensation and rights to some residuals from Good Times. The lawsuits had financial implications as he could not acquire new pitches for script writing.

Eric Monte fell on hard times and filed for bankruptcy in 2003 after developing alcohol and substance abuse that left him addicted. Although he published a book, BluePrint for Peace, copy sales didn't pick up, and he currently resides in a home shelter in Portland, Oregon.

8. Anne-Marie Johnson

Annie Marie Johnson was born on 18th July 1980 in Los Angeles, California, and appeared on the sitcom show for 66 episodes.

After the What's Happening cancellation, Anne was featured on That's So Raven, In the Heat of the Night, and In Living Color. Aside from the shows, she also got regular roles in Melrose Place, JAG, Girlfriends, and The InBetween.

9. (Diane Harris) Debbi Morgan

Debbi Morgan was born on 20th September 1951 in Dunn, North Carolina, and was featured on the ABC sitcom as a recurring cast member from 1976 to 1977. After the cancellation of the sitcom show, she went on to launch a successful acting career and is still active to date.

Some of the films Debbie featured include All My Children, Loving, Generations, Roots: The Next Generations, and The City. In 1989, she became the first African American to win the Daytime Emmy Award for Oustanding Support Actress in a Drama series.

10. (Rob) Earl Billings

Earl Billings was born on 4th July 1945 in Cleveland, Ohio, United States. He is best renowned as Rob in ABC's television series What's Happening. 

He also appeared in the popular Aflac advertisement and posted What's Happening. Earl also appeared in the shows Antwone Fisher, American Splendor, The Incredible Hulk, Something New, and Miss Guided, among others.

Why was What's Happening cancelled?

ABC, the network that aired What's Happening, opted not to renew the series for a fourth season. The decision came after ABC and the cast crew failed to negotiate a pay raise for the team and renew the license agreement despite its consistently good ratings. 

Final word

ABC's What's Happening concluded on 26 March 1988 after three seasons and 66 episodes. Most of the What's Happening cast featured in its sequel, What Happening Now, and went on to work on various projects both in TV and films, although some are also deceased.
