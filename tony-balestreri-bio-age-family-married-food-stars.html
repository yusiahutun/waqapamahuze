Tony Balestreri On Gordon Ramsay’s Food Stars

Instead of a cookery program, Gordon Ramsay’s Food Stars is a hunt for the next great food and beverage sector entrepreneur.

The celebrity chef spoke to Parade.com regarding the show and added, “This is like nothing I have ever done before. I’ve handpicked some of the most promising culinary innovators to compete for not only an investment but my partnership in business. They will have to hustle through tough challenges, break through, set themselves apart from the other competition, and make noise to prove they have what it takes to be successful.”

One such competitor who believes that he has what it takes to become an entrepreneur is Tony Balestreri. According to his bio, he is the Chef/Pitmaster & Founder/CEO of Tony B’s Steak Chips. He has to outdo the other fourteen competitors who are bringing their culinary skills to the show.

The line-up of competitors for the cook-of for the investment opportunity of $250K includes Aaron Valentine, Ashley Davies, Caroline D’Amore, Chanel Goodson, Chris Kanik, Elisa Strauss, Jake Aronskind, Jourdan Higgs, Kagen Cox, Lan Ho, Luther Chen, Megan Meza, Queen Precious-Jewel Zabriskie, and Sydney Webb.

Tony Balestreri Career

Tony Balestreri enjoys cooking over a live fire and eating meat. He has devoted his life to improving the meat-eating experience, from winning whole hog smoking competitions to making specialty sausages.

Tony relocated to Chicago in 2011 and enrolled in Le Cordon Bleu in culinary arts in 2012. He participated in the opening of Red Door in Chicago’s Bucktown district while still in school. He was hired and given the position of sous chef within a year, and he kept accepting new responsibilities to broaden his experience and creativity.

After leaving Red Door, he went on to oversee the new dim sum and brunch program at the highly regarded restaurant Fat Rice. He was inspired to specialize in diverse Asian cuisines and the art of cooking, smoking, and grilling meats with an Asian flair during his time at Fat Rice.

Before accepting the role of sous chef and director of the meat smoking program at Kimski, he worked for a brief period at Goose Island to assist a friend who was the head chef in the catering industry. a hybrid Korean-Polish eatery. The restaurant’s independence and creativity allowed him to routinely experiment with new smoking techniques for standard menu items, specialized products, and special events.

He also worked as the Head Chef at Marz Community Brewing — a newly opened Taproom & kitchen on Chicago’s southside. Starting in 2020, Tony has been employed as Chief Executive Officer at Tony Bs Steak Chips.

Tony received Culinary Arts/Chef Training at Le Cordon Bleu College of Culinary Arts, Chicago. He completed his diploma education at Dominican HS.
