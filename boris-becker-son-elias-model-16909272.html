Boris Beckers catwalk star son calls tennis legend dad a model parent

Hunky Elias Becker, 19, was snapped up by a top modelling agency after moving from America to London last year.

And he credits his former tennis champ father for keeping him on the straight and narrow with early morning workouts and twice-daily yoga sessions.

German Boris shot to fame when he became the youngest man ever to bag a Grand Slam title by winning Wimbledon aged just 17.

And he is drilling his son in his own daily regime with meditation and "goal setting" scheduled into every part of the teenager's life.

Elias, a film student at Roehampton University in south-west London, revealed that since moving into 51-year-old Boris's mansion in the capital he has been starting every day with 50 push-ups at 7am.

"He gives me advice and he gives me routines every day," the aspiring model said.

He tells me what he did when he was my age and I just copy his schedule."

He added: "So I wake up at 7am and I do 50 push-ups and then I go back to sleep for an hour.

"Then I wake up and I do yoga for 30 minutes and then meditation with my dad and then we eat breakfast and we talk about our goals for that day. At night it's a similar thing."

Elias, whose mother is Boris's ex-wife Barbara, has shunned a love life so he can focus on his career.

Despite his good looks, the model, actor and DJ has pledged to stay single.

He said: "I don't have a girlfriend because I don't think it's necessary. I'm too distracted right now. Maybe I'll get a girlfriend in a year or two, who knows?"
