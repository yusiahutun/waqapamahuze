Laverne Cox bio: transition, net worth, partner, brother, movies and TV shows

Laverne Cox is a household name in the world of entertainment. She is a four-time Emmy-nominated actress who doubles up as a dancer and LGBYQ supporter. Cox is known for playing Sophia Burset in Orange Is the New Black (OITNB), Candace in Law & Order: Special Victims Unit, and Cameron Wirth in Doubt. Also, she has been featured by various magazines for her remarkable work in fighting for LGBTQ rights. Some of those magazines include Time, Vogue, Huffington Post, Out, and Essence.

Growing up as an LGBTQ child is not an easy experience. Often, such people are forced to isolate themselves because of the fear of being rejected. That was not any different from Laverne Cox, a transgender male. She had a troubled childhood and teenage experience that almost saw her contemplate murder. Thanks to Candis Cayne role in Dirty Sexy Money, Laverne realized that she could achieve her dreams despite her gender.

Laverne Cox biography

She was born on 29th May 1972 in Mobile, Alabama, United States. Laverne Cox's birth name was Roderick Laverne Cox. She was a male at birth. As of 2021, Cox is 48 years old; she is a few weeks away from turning 49 years old. The name of her mother is Gloria Cox, while the name of her father remains a mystery.

She has a twin brother by the name of M Lamar. Laverne Cox's brother is also in the showbiz scene. The two siblings were raised by a single mother with the help of their grandmother. Unlike Laverne Cox's twin, Laverne had a challenging childhood experience. Often, she was bullied and laughed at by her school mates because she was transgender.

Education

.The 48-year-old was an A-straight student. First, she attended the Alabama School of Fine Arts for her high school studies.

Later, she proceeded to Indiana University Bloomington, where she majored in dance. Finally, she switched to Marymount Manhattan College to pursue acting.

Gender transitioning

Laverne Cox before transition experience was, not the best. In third grade, she was nearly injected with testosterone to make her more masculine than feminine. Deep down her heart, she wanted to be a female, which her mother did not approve of at first.

Like Caitlyn Jenner and Andreja Pejic, she openly spoke about her transition. In many instances, she was offended by people who misgendered her even when she wore a dress and braids. The thought of transitioning crossed her mind in her late teenage years.
