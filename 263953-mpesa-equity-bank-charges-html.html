M-Pesa to Equity bank charges

It is absolutely free to deposit from M-Pesa to Equity bank. However, you should know that the charges are levied by the sending telecommunication company (in our case, by Safaricom). The amount of M-Pesa to Equity charges fully depends on the money transaction sum. So, how can you find out how much money will you have to pay for financial transfer?

Safaricom has provided its users with a convenient Safaricom M-Pesa charges calculator. It is a simple tool, that will help you to calculate any amount of M-Pesa to Equity bank account charges, ATM charges etc. Safaricom M-Pesa Charges Calculator can be found either on the official Safaricom website or on the other Internet sources.

If you visit safaricom.co.ke, in the right bottom corner you will find a small window "Get M-PESA Rates", right near "Find Your Bank Code". In the drop-down list called "Transaction" choose the needed option:

  • Withdrawal from M-Pesa agent;
  • Transfer from unregistered User;
  • Transfer to other M-Pesa users;
  • ATM withdrawal.

Right under the transaction drop-down list, you will find another important field - "Enter Amount". Here you will have to type the amount, that you are going to send via M-Pesa to Equity bank. At the bottom you will find a big dark grey button called "Find Rates". Click on it. In a second you will have the M-Pesa to Equity account charges.

For example, you want to send 2000 Ksh via M-Pesa to Equity bank. With the help of the calculator, choose the needed transaction option and type the sum - 2000 Ksh. As a result, you will get Ksh.73 fee. This means, that to be able to transfer Ksh.2000 you have to have at least Ksh.2073 on your M-Pesa account.

There are also various websites that offer M-Pesa calculator. However, it is better to use the official one at Safaricom, as they always update data, if the fee rate was increased or decreased.

Don't forget that there are minimum and maximum amounts of money that you can send via M-Pesa. For example:

  • To send money to a Registered User, you will have to send at least 10 Ksh.
  • The minimum amount of money that can be sent to an Un-registered User is 101 Ksh.
  • The maximum amount that you can send to an unregistered M-Pesa user is Ksh.35,000. This limit was set by Central Bank of Kenya, to avoid money laundering with the help of different mobile services, such as M-Pesa or Equitel. So, make sure that you are sending money either to a registered user, or less than Ksh.35,000.
