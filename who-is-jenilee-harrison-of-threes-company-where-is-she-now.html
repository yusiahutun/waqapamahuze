Who Is Jenilee Harrison Of Threes Company, Where Is She Now?

Raised as a real Californian girl, the famous actress Jenilee Harrison developed an interest in the world of entertainment at a young age. She dropped out of modeling and walked the ramp for many shows; notable examples include Miss Los Angeles, Miss San Fernando Valley, and Miss Young America.

As she was blessed with a stunning appearance and a perfectly wrapped body, she facilitated her rise to fame, and subsequently, she was made the face of several brands and appeared in many commercials. Between her appearances in commercials and as a model, she attended acting school to hone her skills as a professional actress. Her big breakthrough came on the set of the popular ABC sitcom titled Three’s Company. Jenilee’s role in the sitcom paved the way for her cameo appearances as well as guest roles in several super-hit television series. She starred in the television show known as Dallas and also appeared in a movie and TV mini-series. The actress has also made a name for herself as a voiceover artist in several commercials.

Who Is Jenilee Harrison Of Three’s Company?

Jenilee Harrison was born in Northridge, California, United States of America, on June 12, 1958. She came from a middle-class family, with her father working as an aerospace engineer while her mother earned her living as a math teacher. Although the identity of her parents is not known, according to records, her marriage ended in divorce when the aspiring actress just turned 15. Nevertheless, she remained very close to both parents, especially her mother, to whom she donated a beach house in Malibu, which she bought with part of the money she earned at Three’s Company Show in 1976.

The actress spent her formative years in the San Fernando Valley at the side of her four siblings, consisting of two younger brothers and two older sisters, whose identities were never published. At the young age of 14, she took a paid job and worked as a waitress in her spare time. Jenilee Harrison also won several beauty contests with her beautiful blonde appearance.

As for her education, available records indicate that she is a graduate of the University of Southern California (USC), where she paid her tuition from the proceeds of her work in television commercials and modeled for brands such as Kellogg’s Corn Flakes box. During her graduation year at USC, Jenilee Harrison earned the title of Sweetheart of Sigma Chi; in 1980 she graduated with a degree in Education.

Career

Her career began professionally when she joined the set of Three’s Company – an ABC show she joined in 1980. Jenilee was chosen as a replacement actress for Suzanne Somers, who played the role of Chrissy Snow, the sitcom blonde, but left after the start of the fifth season. Jenilee was cast as Chrissy Snow’s cousin Cindy Snow, who looked very different from her stupid blonde cousin; she looked more like an extremely clumsy farm girl.

Jenilee Harrison found herself on the set of the 1984 Dallas drama in which she played the role of Jamie Ewing Barnes; she appeared alongside actors such as C. Thomas Howell and James Garner, who played the role of a reluctant prostitute in the film called Tank. In 1985 she appeared as a cast member of Super Password. She appeared on the show two more times – the next year and in 1987.

Jenilee appeared briefly in “Lovin’ Every Minute of It” – a music video of a loverboy who gave her a role as a waitress. In 1991, we saw her in a lead role alongside Christopher Lee in the film called Panga, while years later, in 1999, she appeared as a guest artist on the That ’70s Show. Jenilee has worked on TV commercials, the Sobakawa Pillow, the AB Flex, and the Ultimate Chopper with Chef Tony. Jenilee Harrison’s time with the Infomercials lasted from 1996 to 2005.

Where Is She Now?

Jenilee Harrison stopped her acting career in 2002 but still performs sporadically at autograph sessions, conventions, and meetings with fans. According to available records, she has been heavily involved in real estate investment and is now very successful in this business, writing, directing, and producing commercials, and her hobbies include rodeos and car racing.
