Doctors for country music star Randy Travis on Monday said his medical condition has stabilised after suffering a stroke while being treated for congestive heart failure.

The 54-year-old singer remained on a ventilator to help him breathe, but was being weaned from it, said Dr. Gary Erwin of The Heart Hospital Baylor Plano in Texas.

Travis was awake, alert and interacting with his family and friends, Erwin said.

Doing better: Doctors for country music star Randy Travis said on Monday that his medical condition had stabilised after singer suffered a stroke while being treated for congestive heart failure

Medical update: The singer's doctors and his fiancee Mary Davis appeared in a press conference to give an update  on the singer's condition

Travis was admitted to Baylor Medical Center at McKinley on July 7 through the emergency room and was placed on life support, said Dr. Michael Mack.

He was transferred to The Heart Hospital and required mechanical devices to support his heart and circulation for 48 hours.

Travis's heart responded well and he was weaned from that support, but suffered a stroke.

Heart condition: Dr. Michael Mack of The Heart Hospital said drugs and alcohol did not cause Travis' heart condition

Doctors performed emergency neurosurgery to relieve mounting pressure on his brain and the operation was successful, Mack said.

Travis was diagnosed with idiopathic cardiomyopathy, which means he has scarring of the heart muscle that weakens its ability to support circulation.

A biopsy of the heart muscle showed scar tissue and it appears that neither alcohol nor drugs caused his heart condition.

Active and interacting: Dr. Gary Erwin said Travis was awake and had months of stroke recovery ahead of him

'Mr. Travis does have a family history of cardiomyopathy and it is more likely related to that,' Mack said.

Travis likely will need to stay at The Heart Hospital for another two to three weeks to stabilise his heart and begin physcial therapy, Erwin said.

'We anticipate it will take months to recover from the stroke,' he said.

Randy's fiancee, Mary Davis, sat between the doctors and said she was grateful for the 'outpouring of love and affection that has shown up at the hospital and at home and through several different avenues of support.'
