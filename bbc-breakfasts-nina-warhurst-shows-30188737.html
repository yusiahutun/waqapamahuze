BBC Breakfasts Nina Warhurst shows off baby bump after breaking silence on due date

Pregnant BBC Breakfast's Nina Warhurst has shared a first look at her bare bump after sharing an exciting baby update.

The journalist, 42, who is currently expecting her third child with husband Ted, has been glowing throughout her pregnancy journey so far.

And with over 20k followers on Instagram, Nina is no stranger to keeping fans updated on the brand new chapter in her life.

On Thursday (June 8) Nina took to her Instagram Story to share a candid snap of her baby bump for the first time.

In the pic, Nina could be seen lying on her bed with her two sons Michael and Digby - with the latter touching his mum’s stomach.

She captioned the candid post: “Sharing secrets”.

It comes after Nina revealed earlier this week that her due date is just around the corner.

On Monday (June 5), the brunette beauty told fans her new bundle of joy could arrive in a matter of weeks.

On Twitter, fans showered her with compliments following the breakfast show.

They wrote: “Love your dress, when is baby due? You must be due soon. My eldest was a June baby and it was really hot when I had her back in ‘83.”

Nina replied: “Aww and your baby is turning 40! Not long now – am coping with the weather thank you” which she signed off with a kiss.

She announced her third pregnancy back in March as she marked Mother’s Day with a touching post on Instagram.

Sharing a carousel of snaps documenting her pregnancy, including one where she cradled her growing bump, the broadcaster shared her experience so far.

It read: “I’d like to say, ‘You got this Mama!’ But maybe you haven’t. I dunno. It’s hard, isn’t it?

“That said we are doing it again. It’s been a funky few months... involving intense migraines, vomiting, morning kebabs, first-time acne, small acts of violence against fruit, afternoon kebabs, tears, spontaneous snoozes, anxiety and more kebabs.”

She added: “I am settling into the middle chunk (emphasis on chunk) and enjoying the wonders of Mother Nature and her tiny kicks and flutters.

“The boys and the cat have been enjoying their new chair. God willing, Baby Doner-Shish joins the outside world in the summer.”
