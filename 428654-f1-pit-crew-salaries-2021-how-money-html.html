F1 pit crew salaries in 2021: How much money do they make?

Pit crews are an integral element of any Formula One race, and they can make or break their teams. These individuals are responsible for the general maintenance and service of cars while they are racing. Racing enthusiasts have been eager to discover the F1 pit crew salaries and how much money they make per engagement.

The effectiveness of a pit crew is dependent on several factors, including skill, speed, and physical fitness. The current record for the fastest pit stop stands at 1.82 seconds set at the Brazilian Grand Prix by Red Bull in 2018. So, what are the F1 pit crew salaries?

F1 pit crew salaries

Pit stop teams are divided into several sub-departments, each with specific roles to get the work done in the shortest time possible. Each unit has 20-25 members, who are paid according to the task they perform. However, the payment is not the same for all teams, and some pay contract-based salaries to the staff.

Several netizens have also been asking, "Is F1 pit crew a full-time job?" Crew members have full-time jobs in their racing team as mechanics, drivers, and engine fitters, among others. Only the crew chief has a fixed role, but all the others can change on a need basis. So, how much do pit stop crew get paid?

1. Pit crew chief

The chief is the crew boss or the person in charge of all the members. He or she selects his team members and coordinates all the pit stop activities. For example, a Formula One crew chief determines which tyres go on the car and decides on the best strategy for the team.

Because of his crucial role, the team leader is the best-paid member of an F1 pit crew, earning approximately $10,000 per race and $1 million annually. The chief also takes home a race win bonus of roughly $5,000.

2. Tyre changers

Pit crews have a four-member tyre changing team, each responsible for changing one tyre. Tyre changers need to have speed and the skill to ensure they fit the tyre perfectly to avoid mishaps during the race.

These team members take home an estimated $5,000 per race and $350,000 annually. In addition, their race-winning bonus is $2,500.

3. Tyre carriers

Each team has two carriers assigned to each wheel to replace the old tyres with new ones. One of them takes off the wheel while the other one attaches the fresh one. The eight-member team has to be fast to get the wheel to the vehicle in the shortest time possible.

The salary for a tyre carrier is approximately $3,500 per race and an annual salary of $270,000. Carriers also earn an extra $2,500 as a race-winning bonus.

4. Jackmen

Two jackmen lift the car at the back and front using a lever for the pitstop procedure. They then bring it back down once the maintenance operations are complete. Jackmen should be physically fit to endure the strength required to lift the car under intense pressure.

These individuals earn an estimated $3,000 per race and $150,000 per year. Their race-winning bonus is $500.

5. Wingmen

The role of wingmen is to adjust the front wing angle. The wing is the part of the vehicle that first hits the air, shaping the car's aerodynamics. It may require adjusting due to track conditions, driving style or tyre wear. They also change the wing if it needs complete replacement.

Each crew has two wingmen who earn around $3,000 per race and $150,000 annually. Their bonus for winning a race is $500.

6. Stabilizer

Two pit crew members are responsible for stabilizing the car from left, right, and centre. These individuals earn approximately $2,500 per race and $90,000 annually. Their bonus for winning a race is $250.

7. Lollipop man

The lollipop man gives the signal for the car to be released once the pit procedure is complete. An electric signal indicates that the vehicle can be released, and the starter man finds a safe gap in the pit lane traffic, ensuring the driver does not impede another car on exit.

Unlike the other roles, the lollipop man's position is permanent and is held by the chief mechanic. This F1 mechanic salary is approximately $10,000 per race and $800,000 annually. In addition, his race-winning bonus is around $500.

8. Fire extinguisher man

This individual stays on standby to put out a fire in case such an incident occurs. They earn approximately $500 per race and $30,000 per year. Their bonus for a winning race is $250.

Before 2010, the pit crews had a refuelling person who used to refuel the vehicle. However, the new Formula One regulations in 2010 stated that cars should not be refuelled mid-race, so this role no longer exists. Refuellers earned approximately $5,000 per race and $350,000 annually. In addition, their race-winning bonus was $2,500.

Are F1 pit crews engineers? Not all of them are. Most of them are mechanics employed by their racing teams. However, the F1 engineer salary is also attractive.

A Formula 1 engineer analyzes data and makes informed decisions about the required setup to ensure the best car and driver performance. The engineer also conducts pre-briefings, and post-race debriefs with the driver to get crucial information needed when designing vehicle dynamics.
