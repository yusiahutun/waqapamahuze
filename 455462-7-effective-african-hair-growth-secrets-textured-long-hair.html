1. Braid your hair

One of the Fulani hair growth secrets is braiding. Once you have treated your hair, you can braid it into a protective style. You can style braided hair differently and also add hair accessories to it. It helps hair to grow because there is minimal manipulation.

Braids are common in African countries like South Africa, Nigeria, and Ethiopia. When you braid your hair, ensure it is not too tight as it could weaken your hair and damage it.

2. Use Chebe powder to strengthen your strands and treat your scalp

Chebe is a powder that was used by women in the Northern African country, Chad. The product contains shebe seeds, cloves, samour resin, missic stone and mahllaba seeds.

Women who have used Chebe for African hair growth recommend the product because it also has added benefits like moisturizing and strengthening the hair. It also defines your curls. In addition, each of the products in Chebe powder has unique benefits. For example,

3. Use dairy butter to moisturize and strengthen your hair

What stimulates black hair growth? One of the sure hair care tips that help African hair grow is keeping it moisturized. One of the products that can help you achieve this is dairy butter.

This method was common in African countries like Ethiopia and Chad. Besides moisturizing, the butter also strengthened their hair, reducing breakage. The women would apply it every three days and then wash it off after about three weeks.

4. Try dreadlocks for enhanced growth

Locs have gained massive popularity over the past couple of decades. They were common among tribes that used red ochre on their hair, like the Hamar from Ethiopia and the Himba tribe from Namibia.

Dreadlocks bind the hair together, allowing it to grow freely as it also retains moisture. In addition, dreads reduce hair manipulation and pulling, which gives it more room to grow. You can style dreadlocks any way you wish, allowing for creativity without damaging your hair.

5. Use the Omutula tree bark extract to strengthen your hair roots

You might be wondering, ''What did ancient Africans use for hair growth?'' One of the products used by Namibian women was the Omutula or baobab tree bark. The baobab tree is rich in Omega 3, 6, and 9, making it an excellent hair moisturizer. The Omutula bark also strengthens the hair. To apply, grind the bark and mix with fat.

6. Try ghee butter to moisturize and lengthen kinky hair

Netizens have wondered, ''What do Africans put on their hair?'' One of the growth secrets is ghee butter which has been popular in Ethiopia for centuries. It is an excellent option to moisturize, strengthen, and seal in moisture on kinky hair.

7. Apply almond and castor oil for maximum growth

This combination was used in Egypt to enhance hair growth. The castor oil thickens the hair, while the almond oil lightens the mixture and gives the mixture a pleasant smell. Almond oil also reduces hair breakage because it is rich in Vitamin E. You can also mix almond oil with olive or coconut oil to keep your hair moisturized.

Women from different countries have used these African hair growth secrets for centuries. As you try them out, ensure to maintain your regular hair care hygiene, keep your hair clean and moisturized, and avoid heat treatment.
