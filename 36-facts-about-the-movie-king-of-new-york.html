FAQs

1. Is “King of New York” based on a true story?

No, “King of New York” is not based on a true story. It is a work of fiction that explores the criminal underworld of New York City.

2. Who directed “King of New York”?

The movie was directed by Abel Ferrara, a renowned filmmaker known for his gritty and provocative style.

3. When was “King of New York” released?

“King of New York” was released in the United States on September 28, 1990.

4. Who stars in “King of New York”?

The film features an ensemble cast including Christopher Walken, Laurence Fishburne, David Caruso, Wesley Snipes, and Giancarlo Esposito.

5. What is the main plot of “King of New York”?

The movie follows the story of Frank White, a drug lord who seeks to regain control of the criminal empire he once ruled while facing opposition from law enforcement and rival gangs.

6. Does “King of New York” have any notable awards or nominations?

Although the film did not receive major award recognition, it has gained a cult following over the years and is highly regarded by cinephiles.

7. Can you recommend similar movies to “King of New York”?

If you enjoyed “King of New York,” you might also like “Scarface,” “Goodfellas,” “The Departed,” or “New Jack City.” These movies explore similar themes of crime, power, and ambition.

8. Is “King of New York” suitable for all audiences?

No, “King of New York” is rated R for its graphic violence, strong language, and adult themes. Viewer discretion is advised.
