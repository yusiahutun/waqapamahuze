Floravere introduces made to order luxury wedding dresses

A new bridal website has launched that promises to revolutionise the way women buy their wedding dresses.

The current process for buying a luxury bridal gown is often a lengthy, arduous experience involving appointments (usually at inconvenient, mid-week times), cryptic pricing information (prices available upon request) and long waiting times before the dress finally arrives.

However, Floravere aims to be different, with an online made-to-order luxury wedding-dress service, which delivers the gown straight to your home. Fashionista reports that bride-to-bes can shop between seven dress styles – from the J Capulet, a romantic off-the-shoulder gown, to the H Golightly, an elegant full-skirted dress with a plunging neckline.

Floravere

The H Golightly dress

They can have up to three sample dresses at $45 each (these will be credited back with the final dress purchase) sent to their home to try on in the comfort of a bedroom or living-room. The samples need to be posted back within three days with a note containing their favourite style and any customisation details – such as lowered waistline, train length, sleeve length and monogramming.

Floravere

The S O\'Hara dress

The dress is then made to your specifications and will be sent to you within three months, rather than the six-to-nine month wait of most traditional bridal shops. Obviously, this means that the final customised gown can't be returned, but Floravere tries to accommodate any last-minute amendments. The dresses are all made in New York with prices under $4,000 (£3,200).

The idea is the brainchild of Molly Kang, who came up with the concept for her Stanford Business School application essay. She spoke to over 100 brides about the most painful parts of choosing a wedding dress, then worked with the luxury bridal specialists Monique Lhullier, Reem Acra and Carolina Herrera to create the final seven dress designs.

See the full collection of wedding dresses at Floravere.com.
