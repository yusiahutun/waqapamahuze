The expert funnywoman has made quite the ironic meal out of her Emmy's nip-slip incident.

So of course when Tina Fey hosted the season premiere of Saturday Night Live this week, it was only appropriate for her to give herself a quick and comical physical once-over the moment she took to the stage, to make sure everything was in its right place.

The 30 Rock showrunner and star was stunning in red as she graced the stage she worked on for nine years, before welcoming the six brand new cast members of the popular sketch show.

There they are! Tina Fey poked fun at her nip-slip once again as she hosted the season premiere of Saturday Night Live this week, where the new cast members were introduced

Tina was elegantly clad in a chic red dress with puffed sleeves and her hair was styled in graceful curls as she made her entrance for the opening monologue.

Quite literally the first thing she did was reach for her chest and jokingly check that here had been no repeat of the accidental dress slippage that occurred at the Emmy Awards.

Since the brief dress slippage happened, the comedic genius has made light of the incident and even referred to it by flashing her entire chest in an SNL promo video earlier this week.

Making light of it: The seasoned funnywoman dryly poked and prodded at her chest as she took to the stage, jokingly making sure everything was in its right place

The cast of the 39th season! The fresh new featured players stood beside Tina Fey

The comedic genius, who just won an Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing in a Comedy Series, also made light of the fact that she no longer has a show currently on television, joking that this means she now has trouble getting 'the best table at Subway' on account of this.

Tina then went on to introduce the six fresh new Saturday Night Live cast members.

Joining the line-up for the sketch show's 39th season are Beck Bennett, John Milhiser, Kyle Mooney, Mike O'Brien, Noel Wells and Brooks Wheelan, all cast by SNL legend Lorne Michaels to represent the next generation in SNL comedy.

Welcome! Tina introduced the new featured players of SNL, including Beck Bennett, John Milhiser, Kyle Mooney, Mike O'Brien, Noel Wells and Brooks Wheelan

The man himself: SNL's head honcho Lorne Michaels (L) made a rare appearance alongside Tina Fey and Kenan Thompson on Saturday night

Tina appeared to have a whale of a time making the new featured players do a 'humiliating dance', in which she took on the role of boss lady, initiating the freshers.

Though the stunning brunette comedian is no longer a cast member, she appeared in every one of the night's sketches alongside the new actors.

The first sketch of the night was a parody of Lena Dunham's award-winning HBO series Girls, in which Tina played fictional new character 'Berta' and new castmate Noel Wells did a hilarious portrayal of Hannah.

Tradition: The comedic genius had the cast do a deliberately humiliating dance as an initiation

Sorry, Lena: They did a sketch parodying Girls, in which new cast member Noel Wells played Lena's character Hannah and Tina Fey played a hilarious new addition named 'Berta'

More of the new players were introduced when Kenan Thompson put on a 'gameshow' in which Tina's task was to guess whether certain people were new cast members or members of the band Arcade Fire - the band performing on the night.

This was when the show's head honcho, Lorne Michaels, made a rare appearance, pretending in good humor that he didn't recognise the very cast members he hired.

And in yet another particularly topical guest appearance, Breaking Bad's Aaron Paul showed up in a sketch that pretended to advertise 'e-meth', an e-cigarette for meth.

The spot was timely given that the popular series is having its finale tomorrow night.

Brave territory: The popular HBO series was a bold place to tread when it comes to parody but it was done well

A guest! Aaron Paul from Breaking Bad made a guest appearance on the show, the night before the series finale of the popular Sunday night drama

Weekend Update returned with new female anchor Cecily Strong sitting alongside veteran comic Seth Meyers, and Tina hilariously gave her a pep talk before scooting off and leaving them to it.

The much discussed Emmy wardrobe malfunction happened when Tina arrived on stage with her co-writer Tracey Wigfield to accept the award for Outstanding Writing in a Comedy Series.

The accidental flash turned out to be a blink-and-you'll-miss-it moment, but enough to go viral, with fans commenting that the mother-of-two's stunning electric blue gown may not have been tight enough around the cleavage area.

Newcomers: Popular segment Weekend Update welcomed its new female anchor Cecily Strong, who sat alongside SNL veteran Seth Meyers as Tina pretended to school her on the job

Next level: Tina got down to the nitty gritty in this sketch in which she played an ex pornstar advertising expensive items

Earlier this week, Tina truly made light of the 'nip slip' for a promo video to plug her hosting stint on this episode of Saturday Night Live.

The promo sees SNL cast member Kenan Thompson tells viewers that 'there will be no more wardrobe malfunctions like at the Emmys,' and then turns to Tina, asking: 'Unless you want to do one?'

'No that's right,' Tina says firmly as her black jacket falls open to reveal she's topless. 'It's not going to happen again. I've got it under control.'

Realising her 'error,' she wraps the jacket around herself, telling Kenan: 'I forgot my shirt.'

Whoops! Tina Fey endured an embarrassing nip-slip during the televised Emmy Awards last weekend but has managed to make light of it ever since
