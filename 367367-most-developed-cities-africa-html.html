10 most developed cities in Africa to visit and live in 2024

Residing in a developed city is the desire of most people. Developed cities have great road networks, electricity connections, and other ultramodern social amenities. This desire has compelled many Africans to move abroad to countries such as the United States of America, England, Germany, etc. However, unknown to these groups is the fact that the most developed cities in Africa are excellent places to reside in. They have a booming economy, security, and numerous opportunities for career growth.

The African continent is home to 54 countries with some of the best cities to explore and live in. They are culturally rich, eclectic, vibrant, and have modern infrastructure. They also have the warmth of the native's hospitality and friendliness.

Most developed cities in Africa

Africa has cities for everyone, from sprawling and fast pace metropolises to slow-paced cities. Here are the top cities in Africa with incredible housing, infrastructure, weather, security, and holiday destinations.

1. Johannesburg

Which is the most developed city in Africa? Johannesburg is the largest city in South Africa and, indeed, one of the nicest cities in Africa. Jo'burg, as the locals call it, is the provincial capital of Gauteng - the wealthiest province in the country.

It is well developed with modern infrastructures. Expect to experience world-class hotels and skyscrapers that can rival those in major Western cities like New York.

Some of its beautiful attractions include the City Deep - the largest dry port globally and the Apartheid Museum. It’s also one of the cities that hosted the 2010 FIFA World Cup tournament.

2. Cairo

Cairo, a bustling and chaotic city, is the capital of Egypt. It's known as the city that never sleeps – something always happens in this metropolis. It's among the most advanced cities in Africa, and travelers visit it to explore and learn about Egypt’s long-storied history.

Some of the top attractions in this city include Tahir Square and the Museum, which showcases millennia's worth of artifacts and antiquities. Also nearby is Giza which has Egypt's most popular attractions; the iconic pyramids from the 26th century BCE.

3. Casablanca

Casablanca is Morocco's most populous city, with a mix of grit and style, much like Los Angeles. Casablanca is among the modern cities in Africa and is best for travelers who like to feel like locals rather than tourists. It's an excellent place for visitors who want to experience the present and learn about the country's past.

This city is a hotbed of fascinating architecture that will geek you; it has diverse building styles. You'll also enjoy the city's historical art and buzzy restaurants fueled by locals and visitors.

4. Cape Town

Cape Town, South Africa, is one of the nicest cities in Africa and the entire world. It has a mix of natural beauty, which includes the 6,000-foot table-shaped mountain, a string of white-sand beaches, and an incredible atmosphere. The city is culturally rich and has many galleries, museums, and other historical attractions.

When it comes to shopping and dining, there are excellent contemporary shops and restaurants. In addition, Cape Town is also home to more billionaires than other cities in Africa. Its significant sectors are telecommunication, retail, financial service, and tourism.

5. Nairobi

Nairobi, Kenya's capital city, is among the most developed cities in East Africa. It has grown from a rail depot in 1899 to a city of millions. Today, it is the finance, business, and cultural hub of Kenya and East Africa. Expect to see a mix of impressive skyscrapers and always busy people.

In addition, another remarkable facet of Nairobi is its accessibility to African wildlife safaris. Nairobi is famous as the only capital city that has a national park.

6. Abuja

Abuja, the capital city of Nigeria since 1991, sits in the heart of the country. It is called "centre of unity" because it is central and neutral to religiosity and ethnicity. The capital was built from scratch in the 80s, so it has purpose-marked districts and a seamless transport system.

It's one of Africa's few well-planned capital cities, with monumental buildings, modern architectural designs, and parks. In addition, Abuja is well known for its vibrant nightlife. It has high-end lounges, pubs, live music, and more; you'll surely love them.

7. Durban

Durban is South Africa’s third major city known for its multicultural history. It is located on the Indian Ocean and is among Africa's top African beach destinations. Why? Its sea is warmer and has some great surf breaks.

The best attraction in Durban includes the seafront promenade and the Durban Botanical Gardens. It also hosts the Moses Mabhida Stadium, which hosted the 2010 soccer world cup.

8. Accra

Accra is the capital and most densely populated city in Ghana. The city lies partly on a cliff and spreads northward over the undulating Accra plains.

Despite being in a region susceptible to occasional earthquakes, that has not deterred it from being the most advanced city in Africa, with several classic restaurants, bars, shopping malls, and nightclubs.

The city has a flourishing real estate sector, which explains why it has become a favorite vacation destination for most upper-class Africans.

9. Luanda

On the Angolan coast stands the African metropolis of Luanda. Even before colonial times, it was rich in culture but was greatly influenced by the Portuguese in the 16th century.

Luanda, Angola, has a lot to celebrate in its heritage, from traditions to cuisine. It's a city of change and offers quite much to visitors, including diverse musical culture, colonial architecture, an upscale beach scene, and delicacies.

10. Kigali

Twenty-eight years later, after the genocide that devastated Rwanda's wildlife-filled, hilly paradise, its capital city is becoming one of the hippest destinations. Kigali is one of the safest modern African cities, meaning locals and tourists can happily and freely stroll in the beautiful town after the sun.

This city has many things to do; you can learn about the country's dark history by visiting various memorial places. It also has a thriving art scene; there are almost ten art galleries, the most common being Yego Arts and Ivuka Arts. In addition, you can explore the brilliant cafes with delicious food and beautiful furniture.
