Ommy Dimpoz's bio

Omari Faraji Nyembo is a recording artist and songwriter from Tanzania. He was born on September 12, 1987, to Mr and Mrs Faraji Nyembo. He has become a household name across East Africa and beyond due to his musical prowess. Sources claim that his father abandoned his family when Omari was still a young boy.

The father and son have a strained relationship to date, with his father even stating that Ommy should not attend his funeral once he dies. Mr Faraji said this alluding that his son does not stay in touch with him.

Other details of Ommy Dimpoz's family, like his siblings, if any, are yet to be disclosed. Unfortunately, the singer's mother passed away a few years ago. He wrote a post on Instagram remembering fond memories with her.

Career

Omari started entertaining former schoolmates as early as primary school. The artist also took part in talent searches and performed at weddings. He composed his first song while he was in high school.

The singer's stage name, Ommy Dimpoz, came from 'Ommy,' which was a common nickname for people named Omari. 'Dimpoz' came from his dimples, which normally appears when he smiles.

In 2005, while still in high school, Ommy formed the group West VIP alongside four friends. The singer was also a Top Band member and worked as a backup singer for veteran singer TID. He de-linked from the bands in 2011 to pursue a solo career.

In the same year, he met producer Ali Kiba and KGT, who helped him produce his first single, Nai Nai, a hit that dominated the local airwaves. In the following years, he released many singles and featured artists such as Christian Bella, Vanessa Mdee, and Dully Sykes.

Sickness

In 2018, the Bongo artist temporarily went missing from the music scene. His health deteriorated after he was diagnosed with cancer. When he attended Ali Kiba's wedding, Governor Ali Hassan Joho, who was also in attendance, noticed that he had trouble swallowing his food.

The Governor reached out to Ommy and advised him to seek medical attention. Dimpoz travelled to Kenya to seek medical attention, but the doctors referred him to South Africa for advanced treatment. There, he underwent a major operation to rectify the area from his throat to the oesophagus.
