Career and Professional Life

Professionally, Brandon Avery Perlman is famous as Delroy Edwards. He is a well-known music producer and actor. Brandon is most famous for his albums Like Aftershock, the Rio Grande, Desert Sessions, Wagon Wheels, etc.

Likewise, Brandon Avery is an electronic musician, record producer, and DJ.

He is based in Los Angeles. According to AllMusic’s Paul Simpson, Brandon, aka Delroy Edwards, produces gritty, lo-fi house tracks as well as blown-out, abstract beat tapes.

Brandon is also the owner of the record label, L.A. Club Resource. He additionally runs the underground hip-hop reissue label, Gene’s Liquor.

The Beginning

Brandon Avery was exposed to different genres of music while growing up. Some of these genres include post-punk, industrial, funk, and R&B.

Brandon has also shared that he frequented noise concerts and listened to KROQ-FM artists and acts like New Order, Joy Division, and Depeche Mode throughout this time.

Similarly, Brandon’s parents greatly influenced his early musical development. They also provided him with his first drum kit. He later trained classically in jazz drumming as well.

Likewise, Brandon’s mother was a fan of gangsta rap and dancehall. Hence, she introduced Brandon to underground hip-hop and cassette culture.

Later on, a friend of Brandon’s sister introduced him to house music and also taught him how to operate a drum machine.

2010 Onwards

Brandon moved to New York City in the year 2010. After this, Brandon worked at A1 Records with Ron Morelli. Ron founded the label L.I.E.S.

Brandon then debuted with the EP 4 Club Use Only in 2012 through this label. Following this, Brandon released a string of EPs on the label and also founded his imprint, L.A. Club Resource, in 2013.

The label also issued Brandon’s chopped and screwed mixtape series, Slowed Down Funk, for free download.

Then in 2014, The Death of Rave record label issued Brandon’s Teenage Tapes. It is a compilation of experimental tracks that Brandon recorded back in art school.

The same year, Brandon also debuted in the techno project, DJ Punisher.

Later in the year 2016, Brandon Avery released the 30-track full-length Hangin’ at the Beach. Matt McDermott of Resident Advisor described this project as Brandon’s most realized album to date.

Brandon then followed this with the Rio Grande in 2018. The same year, Brandon returned to L.I.E.S. with the LP Aftershock. It is the musician’s first with the label since 2013.

Brandon additionally collaborated with Dean Blunt on Desert Sessions. It is a collection of experimental synthesizer tracks. Then recently in 2020, Brandon issued his full-length album, Slap Happy, via L.I.E.S.

Musical Style and Criticism

Brandon is famous for employing different genres for his different releases. This includes house, techno, chopped and screwed, Memphis rap, noise, hardcore, and obscure American garage rock.

Despite the variety, Brandon’s music generally characterizes lo-fi production and tape hiss. “Pitchfork” critic Marc Masters thought that Brandon combines the faded ‘80s vibe of Ariel Pink and John Maus with a sharp eye toward what moves bodies on dance floors today.

Similarly, Kevin Ritchie of “Now” regarded Brandon’s work as avowedly anti-pop, anti-digital, and anti-glam in its minimalism.

According to Shawn Reynaldo of “Pitchfork”, classic Chicago house and vintage rap tropes informs Brandon’s aesthetics,

Furthermore, Brandon’s work is often associated with the outsider house scene. His EP 4 Club Use Only is regarded as a defining release of the aforementioned genre.

Likewise, DJ Screw heavily influenced Brandon’s Slowed Down Funk mixtape series and it also featured edits of laid-back Southern rap and ’80s R&B grooves filtered through his hazy, lo-fi sensibilities. Meanwhile, Hangin’ at the Beach is characterized by racing new wave-inspired electronic drums and occasional post-punk basslines that permeate through the record.
