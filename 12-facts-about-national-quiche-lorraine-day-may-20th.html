What is National Quiche Lorraine Day?

National Quiche Lorraine Day, celebrated on May 20th, is a day dedicated to honoring one of the most beloved dishes in French cuisine, Quiche Lorraine. This savory pie, known for its simplicity and delicious taste, combines eggs, cream, and bacon in a pastry crust, making it a favorite for breakfast, brunch, or any meal of the day.

Origins of Quiche Lorraine

  • Quiche Lorraine originated in the medieval kingdom of Lothringen, which the Germans later renamed Lorraine. This region, with its rich history and cultural blend, contributed to the creation of this iconic dish.

  • The original quiche was a simple open pie with a filling of egg, cream, and smoked bacon. Cheese was not a traditional ingredient in the earliest versions, though it has become common in quiches served around the world today.
