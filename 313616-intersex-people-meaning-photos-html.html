Intersex people - Meaning and photos

For the first time in history, Kenya will join nations that have adopted intersex people identity into law. Germany took this system in 2018 to give people a chance to register as either female, male or intersex on passports and birth certificates. Previously, the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) only enlisted citizens as either female or male. However, with prolonged consultations, the intersex marker was introduced in December last year.

Most Kenyan tribes have over the years branded people living with intersex conditions as a taboo case. Others have considered them to be having an abnormality or some deficiencies within their bodies. It has led to almost all of them hiding from the public due to the stereotypes attached to them.

For this reason, most of them cannot be accounted for as they do not give correct details due to stigmatization fear. In many organizations such as banks, ID registration, medical insurance, hospitals, passports, and other vital records, required answers are only male or female. Background of intersex recognition in Kenya

In the year 2009, a particular woman in the country gave birth to an intersex child. It forced doctors to put a question mark on the child’s birth notification papers instead of a gender.

The woman went to the court to have three things clarified. Identify documents for the child so that he/she could attend school, a law to prevent surgery on intersex infants unless it is medically a necessity, and a thorough information/psychological support for parents to such kids. Five years later, in a landmark ruling, the court ordered the Kenyan government to issue all the necessary documents to the child, including a birth certificate.

The High Court also ordered the Attorney General (AG) of the country to create a committee that would check into ways of providing support to intersex kids.

The committee did its research and handed recommendation to the AG in April 2014. Their recommendations included delaying surgery for intersex children until they could choose for themselves and also doing a robust survey on their numbers. The task force also cited that an i-marker should be included as an intersex identifier that would be used in public documentation.

Intersex definition

Intersex people are persons born with sex characteristics, including chromosome patterns, gonads, and genitals that cannot fit typical binary descriptions of female or male bodies. Such variations may have genital ambiguity, sexual phenotype, and combinations of chromosomal genotype other than the widely known XY for male and XX for female.

Formerly known as hermaphrodites or congenital eunuchs, Intersex is a term used to describe wide ranges of natural bodily variations. In some cases, intersex characteristics can be visible on birth while in others, the traits can’t be identified until the child reaches puberty. It is because not all chromosomal intersex variations are physically apparent to all the kids.

Biologically, sex is determined by several traits and factors present at birth. They include the following:

  • Number and type of sex chromosomes.
  • Type of gonads: Whether its ovaries or testicles.
  • External genitalia.
  • Internal reproductive anatomy, such as the uterus in women.
  • Levels of sex hormones.

People with characteristics that don’t fit in a typical male or female at birth are classified as intersex based on the above tests. Some children might be born with ambiguous external private parts while others obscure internal organs such as ovaries and testes. Others will become aware of their genotype when they undergo genetic testing because it doesn’t manifest on their phenotype. For this reason, thorough tests are done before finally concluding that the infant is an intersex.

According to the UN, between 0.05% and 1.7% of the world population is born with intersex traits.

What causes intersex?

Ambiguous sex organs primarily occur when hormone abnormalities interrupt or disturb the fetus’s developing private parts and organs during pregnancy.

A baby’s sex is determined during conception just after sperms are deposited to the vagina. The mother’s egg contains an X chromosome while the father’s sperm can either have an X or a Y chromosome. If the X chromosome is inherited from the father, then the child is automatically a female. Similarly, for a Y chromosome, the genotype of the child will be a male (One X and one Y).

Male and female organs then develop from the same tissue holding Y or X chromosomes. The process will highly depend on the presence or absence of feminine or masculine hormones.

  • In males, a Y chromosomal region triggers the development of testicles, which in turn will produce the male hormones. Male parts will then develop as a result of the hormones from the fetus testicles.
  • In females, there are no Y chromosomes, so the effects of male hormones will not be felt thus the genitals develop as female.

Nonetheless, a chromosomal abnormality can also determine the baby’s sex extra complex.

This disruption can result in a mismatch between the appearance of private parts and internal sex organs or even the genetic sex (XX and YY).

  • A deficiency/lack of male hormones in a genetically male fetus may result in intersex. Similarly, exposure of male hormones to a genetically female child may cause ambiguous genitalia.
  • Genetic mutations may also influence the sex of the baby and cause intersexuality.
  • Chromosomal abnormalities such as an extra or a missing sex chromosome can also cause ambiguous genitalia.
  • In other cases, the real cause of intersex cannot be determined.

Possible causes of ambiguous female genitalia include the following:

  • Congenital adrenal hyperplasia: Certain forms of the named genetic ailment can cause adrenal glands to produce excess male hormones known as androgens. It leads to intersexuality.
  • Prenatal male hormones exposure: Certain drugs are known to contain male hormones or rather stimulate the production of them. If in-taken during pregnancy, they can cause female genitals to be a bit ambiguous. This case can occur if the mother carrying the child has an imbalance of hormones or a particular genetic disorder.
  • Tumours: A tumour in the mother’s body can produce male hormones leading to intersexuality of a genetically female fetus. However, this rarely occurs.

In male children, the following are the causes of intersexuality:

  • Impaired testicle development: If the fetus has an impaired testis development, then this means male hormones won’t be produced. This will cause ambiguous genitalia.
  • Androgen insensitivity syndrome: Developing private parts tissues don’t respond to make hormones from the testes, thus causing intersexuality.
  • Abnormalities with testosterone or testes: Abnormalities interfere with the testes’ activity and structure, thereby causing functionality problems with the production of testosterone or cellular receptors that should respond to testosterone.
  • 5a-reductase deficiency: This enzyme is known to defect and impair male hormones’ production.

Other rare and complex syndromes can also affect the genotype of the fetus.

People with ambiguous sex organs may have the following difficulties:

  • Infertility: Can intersex people reproduce? Well, this will depend on a particular diagnosis. For instance, genetic females with congenital adrenal hyperplasia can get pregnant quickly if they choose to do so.
  • Cancer risks: Some sex disorders are commonly associated with certain types of cancer risks.
