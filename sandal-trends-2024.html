Sandal Trends 2024 Are All About These 7 Styles

All products and services featured are independently chosen by editors. However, StyleCaster may receive a commission on orders placed through its retail links, and the retailer may receive certain auditable data for accounting purposes.

It’s time to move your knee-high boots to the back of your closet because it’s officially sandal season. Pick a nail polish shade for your pedicure and pick a sandal trend for 2024 to slip on your feet. Whether you plan on spending the summer at a beach, bopping around the city or somewhere in between, this year’s sandal trends include an option for any setting.

If you’re like me and can’t walk in a heel, you’ll want to opt for a more comfortable (but equally chic) kitten heel. With Y2K fashion in full swing, the mini heel is here to stay. The trend just picked up steam on TikTok—and with summer just around the corner, the it-girls of New York City will soon be dusting off their favorite pairs. The kitten heel is the perfect shoe to wear whether you’re going to a formal event, or just want a cute shoe to run around in.

For the well-deserved beach vacation on your calendar, you can count on comfortable slides and fisherman silhouettes to take over your suitcase. Plenty of the vacation sandal options work for day and nighttime activities so you won’t have to double pack.

If you’re a fan of platform boots, rest assured chunky sandals will be just as big (pun intended) this summer. The platform silhouette has been a huge trend this year and summer is the perfect time to experiment with sandals that help you reach new heights.

No matter what you’ll be doing this summer, there are tons of sandals to slay in. Thankfully, 2024 has brought us a plethora of sandal trends—so you’re sure to find your perfect pair.

  • Slingback Sandals

    This summer, if you can’t decide between a ballet flat or a sandal—we have the perfect shoe for you. Slingback sandals are the perfect choice for a chic yet comfortable look. Like kitten heels, they’re easier to walk in than traditional heels. If you’re packing light for your summer getaway, slingback sandals are a necessity because they can be dressed up or down.

    Marcella Greta Sandals

    If you’re going to a formal event this summer and hate heels, these Marcella Greta Sandals are both comfy and cute.

  • Fisherman Sandals

    Fashion inspired by the 2014 Tumblr-era has made its way back into the trend cycle and fisherman sandals are a prime example. Fisherman sandals feature a caged silhouette and are typically made out of a leather material (though you’ll also find rubber jelly iterations). The design makes the shoes comfortable and easy to walk in. They’re especially a great choice if you live in a city and don’t want any part of your foot to get too close to the ground.

  • Kitten Heels

    Kitten heels were huge in the 2000s and they’re making a big comeback this summer. (Carrie Bradshaw would be proud.) Mini heels are certifiably cool again and as someone who hates blisters and high heels, I’m thrilled. The style is getting back to its Y2K roots with tons of options featuring bows, flowers, and butterflies—making them a girly staple for your wardrobe. Wear them with a mini skirt, a maxi dress or breezy jeans for the ultimate summer look.

  • Slide Sandals

    When you’re running out of the house, you don’t want to deal with buckling or lacing a shoe up. Slide sandals are the most effortless sandal option to put on your feet while still looking totally put together. There are so many different versions of slide sandals on the market right now, which means you can either opt for a minimalist black leather pair or go for something fun (I prefer the second option).

    Chinese Laundry Surfs Up Sandal

    The Surfs Up Sandal is both trendy and functional, making it a great choice for the beach.

  • Platform Sandals

    Whether you need extra height or just love stomping around, platform sandals are the it-girl shoe for the summer. They come in a variety of materials and colors making them a very versatile silhouette. Not to mention, they pair perfectly with almost any outfit. While countless designers have released their own version of platform sandals, there are tons of options that aren’t as high end.

  • Mule Sandals

    Mules are the perfect hybrid shoe between platform sandals and slides. Just like slides, you can slip them on in a rush and look effortlessly stylish. ’90s shoe trends will always be cool and these are proof. When in doubt, a mule sandal is always a great shoe to wear out to dinner because it can easily be dressed up or down.

    Melissa Posh Mules

    Melissa is known for its jelly shoes, so these Posh Mules are sure to be a comfy choice.

    A New Day Ricky Platform Heels

    For an affordable leather option, go for the Ricky Platform Heels from Target.

  • Platform Flip Flops

    You read that right. Flip flops are officially chic again—but more specifically, platform flip flops. Like a lot of 2024’s sandal trends, platform flip flops are straight out of the ’90s. They might not be the most practical shoe, but they’re a great choice for hitting the beach or running around on a hot day. Everything’s better in platform so naturally these controversial shoes are making a comeback in a bigger silhouette.

    Crocs Getaway Platform Flip Flops

    Crocs have mastered controversial shoes and these Getaway Platform Flip Flops are no different. Whether you like them or not, it-girls everywhere will soon have a pair.

    Rocket Dog Crush Wedge Flip Flop

    A ’90s staple, the Rocket Dog Crush Wedge Flip Flop. These have a huge 3 inch heel making them the perfect chunky shoe for the summer.
