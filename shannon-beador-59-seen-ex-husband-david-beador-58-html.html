Shannon Beador, 59, is seen again with her ex-husband David Beador, 58

Real Housewives of Orange County star Shannon Beador, 59, was seen again with her ex-husband, David Beador, 58.

This is the second time this month they have been pictured together.

On Monday they were side by side as in a photo posted to Instagram.

They were attending their twin 18-year-old daughters Stella and Adeline’s high school graduation as Shannon said she was proud of her girls.

'They have grown into such beautiful young ladies and I can’t wait to watch them in their exciting next chapters,' she wrote. 'Congratulations Stella and Adeline! I love you!!! ❤️❤️.'

This comes after Beador and his wife Lesley, 39, have filed domestic violence restraining orders against each other amid their bitter split and second divorce filing last month.

On June 2 they were seen side by side at night in a photo shared to Instagram. They were at the Orange County hotspot called The Quiet Woman. 

The mother of three snapped the quick selfie in the dimly lit space, and both stars could be seen sporting black ensembles while enjoying their night out. 

In the caption on both the Twitter and Instagram posts, the TV personality penned, 'You never know who you are going to run into the Quiet Woman...' 

Shannon additionally uploaded the photo onto her Instagram stories, and added, '@thequietwomancdm encounter,' followed by the hashtag '#itsbeenyears.'

Fellow celebrities and social media users jumped to the comment section to express their thoughts and surprise from the selfie. 

Shannon's Real Housewives co-star, Tamra Judge, wrote, 'I guess I left too soon,' followed by a wide-eyed emoji. 

Her other castmate, Vicki Gunvalson, typed out, 'Awe your girls will be happy,' referencing to the former couple's three daughters: Sophie, 21, as well as twins Stella and Adaline. 

One fan penned, 'The shock on my face when this showed up on my feed. I thought I skipped a timeline,' while another applauded the photo. 'Good to see you two co-existing. Great for your girls,' followed by a red heart emoji. 

Shannon and David said 'I do' in 2000, and throughout the course of their marriage, welcomed three daughters. 

However, in 2015, the reality star discovered her husband's infidelity and the pair enrolled in couple's counseling in order to patch up and save their marriage. Two years later, the pair announced their separation in October 2017. 

In a statement to BravoTV.com at the time, per Us Weekly, Shannon expressed, 'After much thought and careful consideration, David and I have made the difficult decision to separate.' 

'We remain partners in parenthood and are committed to raising our three daughters. This is not the future we envisioned, and we kindly ask for privacy, especially for our children, during this transitional time,' she added.

Shannon and David promptly went through a bitter divorce and child custody battle, and the two later reached a settlement in April 2019, with the Real Housewives star receiving an award of $1.4 million and also $10,000 a month for support. 

However, the path to the finalization was long, and at one point, David asked the TV personality to stop drinking and stated in documents obtained by The Blast at the time that her 'behavior is detrimental to the children.' 

A source told Us Weekly around the same time that her ex was purposely prolonging the divorce battle. 'David's filing is just another example of how low he can go. Shannon is frustrated and just wants this divorce to be over.' 

The insider further added, 'But he is dragging it out and doing whatever he can so he doesn't have to pay her out.' 

The following year in 2020, Shannon told the entertainment outlet that David still 'doesn't wanna' speak with her although a settlement had been reached. 'They love their dad,' she stated, referencing to their children. 

'I want them to have a relationship with their father. So, you know, I would prefer to have more of a coparent, but he’s not interested. But we’re getting through it.'

Since their split, Shannon began to date John Janssen, but their relationship came to an end in November 2022. While talking to People in January, the Real Housewives star admitted that she was 'blindsided' by the break up. 

'We were in a great place. But seven days later, when the cameras were down, it was a completely different story. He told me he was done with the relationship. And to hear what he said to me then was absolutely devastating.'

She added, 'I was hopeful this was the man I was going to spend the rest of my life with. I wanted it to work out. I've never loved anyone like I've loved John. But clearly, he didn't feel the same way.'

Janssen himself stated that it had been a 'super hard' decision to go forward with the split, but emphasized it was 'the right thing for' both of them.

They  have filed domestic violence restraining orders against each other amid their bitter split and second divorce filing last month.

David and Lesley both claim their orders are necessary due to their alleged behavior toward one another and their two-year-old daughter Anna Love Beador according to court filings obtained by People.

The domestic violence restraining orders serve to protect people from abuse, or threat of abuse, from someone they have a close relationship with. 

Lesley is claiming that she has made the filing and is requesting for supervised visitation for David's alleged 'threats, harassment and hostile behavior' toward their young daughter which caused them 'to fear for our safety and suffer substantial physical and emotional distress.'

In the documents, the wife claimed that she had come to an agreement with her estranged husband that she would be able to use their Laguna Beach rental property until June 30 and that $40K would be given to her to find another home after that date in addition to joint legal and physical custody of their daughter.
