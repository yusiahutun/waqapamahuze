Jeezy's biography

Jay Wayne Jenkins, popularly known as Jeezy, and formerly Young Jeezy, was born in September 1977. Several fans have wondered, ''Where is Jeezy from?'' The rapper was born in Columbia, South Carolina but grew up in Georgia, Hawkinsville, and Macon.

Jay Jenkins' childhood was influenced by gangs and street hustling prompted by financial constraints. His parents went separate ways soon after he was born. Jenkins was raised by his mother and maternal grandmother. He spent a significant part of his childhood staying with various relatives.

Jay's educational background remains a mystery. As a young boy, he got into several money-making ventures. He worked at his uncle's construction company for some time, but he later engaged in illegal activities like selling stolen mobile phones. At 17 years old, the teenager was sent to the Youth Challenge Academy for nine months for possession of narcotics.

Career

Jay Wayne co-founded Corporate Thugz Entertainment (CTE) in 1998 but decided to go solo shortly afterwards. Jenkins released his debut album, Thuggin' Under the Influence, in 2001. His sophomore project, Come Shop Wit Me, came out in 2003.

In 2004, he was signed under Bad Boy Records and became part of the Boyz n Da Hood group. They released their self-titled album in June 2005. Jay joined Def Jam Records in 2005 and released his debut studio album Let's Get It: Thug Motivation 101 in July 2005.

Jeezy's album sales climax at Number 2 on the Billboard 200. In addition, the singles on this collection performed exceptionally well. For example, Soul Survivor, featuring Akon, peaked at Number 4 on Billboard Hot 100. The single is the highest-performing track in his career.
