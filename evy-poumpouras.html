Biography Timeline

Evy Poumpouras is a former Secret Service Special Agent, author, and media commentator. She was born on January 1, 1977, in Queens, New York, and grew up in a Greek-American family. Poumpouras graduated from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign with a degree in criminal justice and later joined the Secret Service, where she served for over a decade. After leaving the Secret Service, Poumpouras became a media personality and author, appearing on several television programs and writing a bestselling book.

1996 – Poumpouras started her college education at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

2001 – She joined the Secret Service and served as a Special Agent for over a decade.

2008 – Poumpouras was selected as one of the first women to serve on the Secret Service’s elite Presidential Protective Division.

2012 – She received the prestigious Secret Service Valor Award for her role in a successful hostage rescue operation.

2018 – Poumpouras retired from the Secret Service and became a media commentator, appearing on several television programs, including CNN, MSNBC, and Fox News.

2020 – She published her first book, Becoming Bulletproof: Protect Yourself, Read People, Influence Situations, and Live Fearlessly, which became a New York Times bestseller.

Poumpouras has received numerous accolades for her service in the Secret Service, including the Secret Service Director’s Award for Excellence and the Department of Homeland Security Secretary’s Award for Excellence. She also received the Ellis Island Medal of Honor, recognising individuals who have made significant contributions to American society. Poumpouras continues to be a respected figure in the law enforcement community and a source of inspiration for women in male-dominated fields.
