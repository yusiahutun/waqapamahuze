Goldie Hawn, 77, is almost unrecognizable as she makes a duck face

Goldie Hawn was pictured walking in the rain in New York City on Friday. 

The blonde beauty, 77, showed off a puffy pout while making a duck face as she clasped a healthy looking purple smoothie. 

The Oscar Winner rocked a stylish black puffer jacket, fitted black pants and an edgy striped shirt tied around her waist.

She flashed a bright red manicure that matched the gemstone in her necklace and sported a chic pair of black slip-on shoes.  

Hawn has been in Manhattan this week enjoying some quality time with her longtime love Kurt Russell, 71, and her daughter Kate Hudson, 43.

One of Goldie's beauty secrets is keeping her skincare routine simple and using the magic of her own hands.

'I wash my face every,' she told Rescu, 'and I massage it for about three minutes, and then I put creams on my face, and I go to sleep.

'And then I wake up in the morning and do the same thing! I don't do much. I was using coconut oil for a while on my face.'

Staying hydrated and protecting her skin from the sun have always been top priorities for the Christmas Chronicles 2 star. 

'I put on a little sun guard in the morning when I remember, and the rest of it is just making sure that you're hydrated and that you eat a lot of oil-based foods or oils and smile a lot.' 

And it's all in the genes for Goldie when it comes to her famous blonde locks, as the First Wives Club star has never colored her hair.

'I know it sounds strange, Hawn told Allure, 'but I've never colored my hair. I just put a regular rinse on it because I don't have any chemical on it.'

Speaking to People in 2017, Goldie described her health and wellness regime that keeps her looking youthful.

'I try to drink my juices. I try to work out every day a little bit. I find moments in the day. I have short meditation spurts during the day. If I don't have a long one in the morning.

'Sometimes the phone rings and you're off and running, but I do find time to get quiet. I hike. I bike. I try to move as much as possible.'

Hawn says that staying 'very, very active' is key to maintaining one's mental health and staying positive. 

'I try to stay positive and be very, very active. I walk every day and I run and do all kinds of that.'

The Bird on a Wire star went on to tell People that she prefers quick, focused workouts as opposed to long grueling gym sessions.

'If I don't have an hour, I'll do 15-minute intervals. Actually, it's a very good thing to do. If you're taxing yourself too much in an hour, you're taxing a lot of things.'

And when it comes to her diet plan, Goldie's core tenets are food separation and not overeating.

The Death Becomes Her star told Porter magazine in 2015 that aging is 'a fact of life' that can be faced by 'living mindfully.'

'Getting older is a fact of live,' she said. 'By living mindfully you understand that there are many transitions in life. you just go through them.'
